UBC On-line calendar description: Trend and seasonality, autocorrelation, stationarity, stochastic models, exponential smoothing, Holt-Winters methods, Box-Jenkins approach, frequency domain analysis.

Prerequisites: Introductory probability, introductory statistics; Corequisite: Stat 305 (Introduction to Statistical Inference); Recommended: Stat 306 or Stat 300 or Econ 326 (Multiple Regression applications without linear algebra).

References
1. Forecasting: Principles & Practice, 2nd edition, by Hyndman and Athanasopoulos; online version at https://otexts.com/fpp2/
2. Reproducible Code, published by British Ecological Society. BES-Guide-Reproducible-Code-2019.pdf (Useful reference for file management for work in team project).

Learning objectives

add to your statistical toolkit for data science applications
get more experience in acquiring data sense in working with data in scientific studies
add to your understanding of probability and mathematics for developing statistical methodology
get coding practice in writing functions for forecast performance measures such a RMS prediction error
acquire a big picture view of a general approach for time series forecasting with different assumptions for probability distributions
understand the George Box quote: All models are wrong but some are useful. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_E._P._Box:
differentiate between a data generating process and a statistical model that is just used for short-term forecasts
